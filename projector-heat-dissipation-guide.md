# Gobo Projector Heat Dissipation Guide: Fin, Cold-Forged, and Copper Pipe Cooling

Heat dissipation is one of the key factors that affects the brightness stability, service life, and outdoor reliability of a gobo projector, logo projector, industrial safety projector, or LED projection light.

Noparde currently uses different heat dissipation structures according to projector wattage and application requirements:

| Projector Range | Current Cooling Structure | Typical Use |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Small-wattage projection lights | Fin heat sink | Compact LED projection lights and lower-power logo projectors |
| 100W projection lights | Cold-forged heat sink | 100W gobo projector and outdoor logo projection applications |
| 300W projection lights | Copper pipe plus inserted-fin heat sink | High-power gobo projector, long-distance logo projection, and industrial safety projection |

## Why Heat Dissipation Matters for Projection Lights

Projection lamps generate heat around the LED light source, optical path, power supply, and housing. If heat is not transferred away efficiently, the fixture may experience brightness drop, shortened component life, unstable operation, or faster aging in outdoor environments.

For projection-based products such as custom logo projectors, water-ripple projectors, rotating gobo projectors, and industrial safety projection lights, a suitable heat sink helps keep the light source stable during long working hours.

## Fin Heat Sink

A fin heat sink increases the contact area between the heat sink and surrounding air. It is usually made by stamping, extrusion, or welding. The main body is combined with multiple thin fins, and some designs may also work with heat pipes for stronger heat transfer.

The basic idea is simple: more fins and smaller fin spacing can increase the effective cooling area, which helps improve air convection.

### Advantages

- Good balance between cooling area and weight
- Suitable for compact projection lamps and small-wattage LED lights
- Easier to produce in quantity
- Cost-friendly for standard product lines
- Flexible surface treatment options, such as painting, plating, or different colors

### Limitations

- Cooling performance depends heavily on fin area, air flow, and housing design
- Painted surfaces may age, crack, or peel after long-term thermal expansion and contraction
- For higher wattage, a fin-only structure may not provide enough heat transfer efficiency

## Cold-Forged Heat Sink

A cold-forged heat sink is formed by cold extrusion as a one-piece structure. It commonly uses high-purity aluminum, such as 1070 aluminum. The forged structure is dense, and the fins can be made thin with narrow spacing.

Compared with split or assembled structures, a one-piece cold-forged heat sink reduces thermal transfer loss caused by joints. This makes it suitable for 100W projection lights where stable heat transfer and compact structure are both important.

### Advantages

- One-piece structure with no assembly gap in the main heat path
- Direct and fast heat transfer from the light source area
- Better cooling stability than many die-cast or ordinary aluminum profile structures
- Can support higher power in a smaller volume
- Suitable for anodized surface treatment with a clean and consistent appearance
- Helps improve product life by reducing internal heat accumulation

### Limitations

- Production process is more demanding than basic stamped or extruded fins
- Not always the lowest-cost option for very small-wattage products
- Best value appears when the projector needs stronger cooling, better appearance, or longer operating life

## Copper Pipe Plus Inserted-Fin Heat Sink

For 300W high-power projection lights, Noparde uses a copper pipe plus inserted-fin cooling structure. The copper pipe transfers heat quickly away from the high-temperature area, while the inserted fins expand the cooling surface for air convection.

This structure is better suited for high-power gobo projectors, long-distance outdoor logo projectors, and industrial safety projection lights that need stable operation under heavier thermal load.

### Advantages

- Strong heat transfer from the LED source area
- Larger effective cooling area through inserted fins
- Suitable for 300W high-power projection lights
- Better for long working hours and outdoor industrial applications
- Helps maintain brightness stability in high-load projection scenarios

### Limitations

- Structure is more complex than small fin heat sinks
- Usually larger and heavier than low-power cooling designs
- More suitable for high-power fixtures than compact low-power products

## Fin Heat Sink vs Cold-Forged Heat Sink

| Comparison Point | Fin Heat Sink | Cold-Forged Heat Sink |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Main principle | Increases surface area and improves air convection | Uses dense one-piece metal structure for direct heat transfer |
| Common process | Stamping, extrusion, or welding | Cold extrusion and one-piece forming |
| Structure | Main body with multiple fins | Integrated body with no major heat-path joint |
| Typical material | High-conductivity aluminum such as 1060 or 1050 aluminum | High-purity aluminum such as 1070 aluminum |
| Cooling strength | Depends on fin area and air flow | Stronger direct heat conduction |
| Surface treatment | Painting, plating, and color options | Often suitable for anodizing |
| Typical application | Small-wattage LED projection lights | 100W gobo projector and outdoor logo projector |
| Main value | Cost control and efficient mass production | Higher performance, compact size, and longer service life |

## How Noparde Selects Cooling Structures

Noparde does not use one heat sink design for every projector. The cooling structure is selected according to wattage, housing size, installation environment, and expected working time.

- Small-wattage projection lights usually use fin heat sinks because they need compact size, lightweight structure, and efficient production.
- 100W projection lights use cold-forged heat sinks because this wattage needs stronger heat transfer while keeping a clean housing structure.
- 300W projection lights use copper pipe plus inserted fins because high-power projection creates more heat and needs stronger thermal transfer plus larger convection area.

## Buyer Questions

### Which cooling structure is better for a 100W gobo projector?

For a 100W gobo projector or outdoor logo projector, a cold-forged heat sink is usually a better match because it provides stronger direct heat transfer and a more compact one-piece structure.

### Why do small-wattage projection lamps use fin heat sinks?

Small-wattage projection lamps do not always need the heavier or more complex cooling structure used on high-power models. Fin heat sinks provide enough cooling area while keeping the product light, simple, and cost-friendly.

### Why does a 300W projection light need copper pipe cooling?

A 300W projection light generates more heat near the LED source. Copper pipes help transfer heat away quickly, and inserted fins provide a larger area for air cooling.

### Does better heat dissipation improve projector life?

Yes. Better heat dissipation can reduce internal heat accumulation, which helps protect the LED source, driver, and optical components during long-term operation.

### Is heat dissipation important for outdoor logo projection?

Yes. Outdoor logo projection often requires long working hours, stable brightness, and weather-resistant housing design. A suitable heat sink helps maintain stable performance in these conditions.
